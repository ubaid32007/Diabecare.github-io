Exercise Guide
Physical activity improves insulin sensitivity
Exercise & Blood Sugar
Check blood sugar before exercising. If below 100 mg/dL, eat a small snack first. Aim for 150+ minutes of moderate activity per week.
🏃 Recommended Activities
🚶
Brisk Walking
30–45 min/day · Lowers blood sugar 20–50 mg/dL
~150 kcal

20–30 min · Cardio with joint safety

⚠️ Safety Rules Before & After
BEFORE EXERCISE
Check blood sugar. Below 100 mg/dL → eat 15g carbs first (banana, juice). Above 250 mg/dL → skip intense exercise.
DURING EXERCISE
Carry fast-acting glucose (tablets or juice). Stop if you feel dizzy, shaky, or confused.
AFTER EXERCISE
Check blood sugar again. Blood sugar can drop 4–8 hours after exercise (delayed hypoglycemia).
HYDRATION
Drink water before, during, and after exercise. Dehydration raises blood sugar levels.
FOOT CARE
Wear proper footwear. Check feet before and after exercise for blisters or sores. Diabetic neuropathy may reduce sensation.

Health Tips
Evidence-based diabetes management advice
🩸 Blood Sugar Control
TIP 01
Check blood sugar at consistent times: fasting, 2h after each meal, and bedtime.
TIP 02
Stress raises blood sugar. Practice 5 minutes of deep breathing or meditation daily.
TIP 03
Sleep 7–9 hours nightly. Poor sleep increases insulin resistance significantly.
TIP 04
Always carry fast-acting sugar (juice, glucose tablets) for hypoglycemia.
🍽️ Smart Eating
TIP 05
Eat vegetables first, then protein, then carbs. This order reduces post-meal glucose by up to 30%.
TIP 06
Eat smaller, more frequent meals. Large meals cause dangerous glucose spikes.
TIP 07
Add cinnamon to your diet — it has been shown to improve insulin sensitivity.
🏥 Medical Care
TIP 08
Get HbA1c tested every 3 months. Target below 7% for most Type 2 diabetics.
TIP 09
Annual eye, kidney, and foot exams are essential for detecting complications early.
TIP 10
Check feet daily for cuts, blisters, or redness. Neuropathy reduces sensation.
⚠️ Emergency Signs
Hypoglycemia <70 mg/dL
Trembling, sweating, confusion. Eat 15g fast sugar immediately.
Hyperglycemia >250 mg/dL
Excessive thirst, frequent urination, blurred vision. Contact doctor.
DKA — Call Emergency Now
Fruity breath, nausea, vomiting, deep rapid breathing.
